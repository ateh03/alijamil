KI #1: The Tip of the Cyber Iceberg

On my first internship, finding my vocation in the cybersecurity rabbithole and learning to trust intuition.

My junior year matriculating at USC, something miraculous happened. I spent weeks that August applying to a myriad of computing internships, to no avail. Seeing as my mantra is “rejection is redirection”, I ceased the grueling application cycle and instead decided to dedicate my time to my role at the Boys & Girls Club and Fall semester classes. Everything changed when a few weeks into September I received a phone call early one Friday morning. The Chief Information Officer (CIO) of the South Carolina Department of Employment and Workforce (DEW) had called me, and stated that I had been selected to participate in DEW’s inaugural cybersecurity analyst internship that had just launched in July, as a member of the second cohort. I gladly accepted, and two weeks later I found myself sitting through the orientation and onboarding process, completely unaware of how much knowledge I would accrue over the six month duration of the program.

Going into my junior year, I had very little knowledge of what I deem the “Cybersecurity Iceberg.” My program curriculum requires CSCE201 “Intro to Computer Security”, which covers basic security implementations users can practice, risk management, basic cryptography and ends with students giving a presentation on a research paper of relevant sectors where cybersecurity plays a major underlying role. As much as I enjoyed this class and its extremely talented lecturer Dr. Neema Kanapala, I didn’t realize just how deep this Iceberg was.
